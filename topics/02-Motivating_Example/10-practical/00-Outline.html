Overview

This week, there are two exercises to do in the lab.

Analysis of student marks using pandas

Last week, you were invited to investigate a very small file containing data on American cars.

This week, please answer the following questions from the data:

  1. What is the average mark per module?
  2. What is the average mark per student?
  3. What are the top two modules per student?
  4. What are the top two students per module?
  5. On average, do students achieve higher marks in exams than in practical (i.e., non-exam) assessments?

Using the python data mining toolchain to classify iris plants

Using the classification techniques we meet this week, you are asked to explore a Jupyter notebook1 that fits the iris data.

This allows you to gain experience with running and editing a nontrivial notebook.

Please follow any instructions and add notes in the markdown cells you are asked to add to the notebook.

  1. Right click and "Save Link As..." or equivalent for your browser, to download as a file. ↩
